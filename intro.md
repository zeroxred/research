# Introduction

This compendium gathers the initial research program developed as preparation for entering a Ph.D. trajectory at TUAT. It serves as a structured working document to organize the motivation, research direction, study plan, and technical foundations required to build a solid proposal in intelligent video understanding.

The central theme of this project is **spatiotemporal deep learning for video surveillance analysis**. The research is motivated by the need for automated systems that can support public safety through the analysis of real-world surveillance video. In particular, the project begins with supervised action recognition for security-related events such as fights and robberies, and progressively moves toward anomaly detection for identifying unseen or irregular behaviors in open-world scenarios.

This book is organized as a gradual roadmap. It starts with the student objectives and the initial research context, then defines the proposed research theme, and finally presents a phased plan that moves from foundational study to practical model development. The progression includes:

- study of spatiotemporal representations for video understanding
- implementation of baseline action recognition models
- integration of object detection and multi-object tracking into a surveillance pipeline
- extension toward video anomaly detection
- exploration of efficient and research-oriented architectures for real-time analysis

The material collected here is intended to support both short-term learning and long-term research planning. It combines conceptual study, dataset exploration, and implementation-oriented notes that will help shape future experiments and, eventually, a more formal academic document.
